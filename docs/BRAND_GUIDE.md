# Brand Guide

## Brand Name

**Aptly**

## Brand Idea

Aptly helps hiring teams understand candidate fit with structure, evidence, and consistency.

The name suggests precision and appropriateness without sounding artificial or overly technical.

## Positioning Statement

Aptly is structured interview infrastructure for modern hiring teams.

## Brand Personality

- Assured.
- Precise.
- Useful.
- Quietly premium.
- Trustworthy.
- Human-centered.

## Logo Direction

The logo should be a refined wordmark with a simple geometric mark.

Recommended symbol direction:

- A restrained monogram built from an `A` and a check-like angle.
- Or a simple aperture/frame shape suggesting structured observation.

Avoid:

- Robot heads.
- Chat bubbles.
- Brain shapes.
- Sparkles.
- Abstract neon symbols.

The logo should work in:

- Black.
- White.
- Single-color ink.
- Small sidebar sizes.

### Usage Rules

- Keep the UI mostly neutral.
- Use Signal Blue for primary actions and active navigation.
- Use Evergreen for positive evaluation and completion states.
- Use amber for monitoring warnings and expiring invitations.
- Use red only for destructive actions or genuine failures.
- Do not use purple as a dominant brand color.

## Typography

Recommended product font:

- Inter.

Recommended optional brand/display font:

- Geist.

Rules:

- Use one primary sans-serif in the product.
- Maintain tight but comfortable hierarchy.
- Avoid expressive display type in operational screens.
- Keep letter spacing at 0.

## Icon System

Use lucide icons.

Icon style:

- 1.5px to 2px stroke.
- Rounded line caps where available.
- Minimal, practical, recognizable.

Preferred metaphors:

- Briefcase for roles.
- User for candidates.
- Clipboard for reports.
- Mail for invitations.
- Shield for security.
- Clock for expiration.
- FileText for transcript.
- Video for recording.
- AlertTriangle for warnings.

Avoid:

- Bot.
- Brain.
- Sparkles.
- Wand.
- Rocket as a primary product metaphor.

## Illustration and Imagery

The product should not rely heavily on illustration. If images are needed:

- Use real, professional workplace photography only in marketing contexts.
- Avoid staged headset-call-center imagery.
- Avoid futuristic AI imagery.
- Avoid abstract glowing backgrounds.

Inside the app, prefer:

- Data tables.
- Timelines.
- Reports.
- Structured review panels.

## Voice and Tone

Voice:

- Direct.
- Professional.
- Calm.
- Evidence-based.
- Human-owned.

Examples:

- "Results are ready for review."
- "This invitation expires on July 7."
- "We detected a brief camera interruption. Review the recording for context."
- "The candidate completed all readiness checks."
- "A reviewer adjusted the score with additional context."
- "This recommendation is based on the rubric and supporting evidence."

Avoid:

- "Our AI has decided..."
- "Magic interview complete."
- "Bot score."
- "Suspicious behavior detected" for normal monitoring warnings.
- "Rejected by AI."
- "Automatically failed."

## Product Naming

Recommended object names:

- Workspace.
- Role.
- Candidate.
- Invitation.
- Interview plan.
- Interview session.
- Transcript.
- Evaluation.
- Report.
- Monitoring warnings.

Avoid:

- Bot.
- Agent interviewer.
- Magic score.
- AI verdict.

## Brand Experience Principles

1. Make evidence easy to review.
2. Keep sensitive decisions human-owned.
3. Treat candidates respectfully.
4. Keep AI invisible where possible.
5. Prefer clarity over spectacle.
6. Present uncertainty honestly.
7. Make privacy, consent, and support access feel trustworthy rather than hidden.

## Enterprise Trust Language

Use calm, specific language for sensitive workflows:

- Support access: "Aptly support accessed this workspace for a time-limited investigation."
- Legal hold: "Retention deletion is paused for records covered by this hold."
- Privacy requests: "This request is being verified before data is exported or changed."
- AI evaluation: "This evaluation supports reviewer judgment and cites the interview evidence used."

Avoid language that implies surveillance, secrecy, or automated hiring decisions.
